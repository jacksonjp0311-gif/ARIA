# Etherflow

Etherflow is ARIA's triadic operator surface. It renders verified runtime events as three coordinated lanes:

- 🜂 energy — what force or phase is moving through the system;
- ∿ information — what payload, artifact, or message is being carried;
- 🜄 coherence — what authority, integrity, or terminal state is currently true.

The renderer is intentionally downstream of semantics and verification. ARIA does not "look futuristic first" and decide later; it decides first, then renders the verified state in a higher signal form.

## Profiles

- `operator` — full-width lane rendering;
- `compact` — compressed line protocol for narrow shells;
- `ci` — noninteractive, single-line compressed output for automation;
- `ether` — full triadic stream for interactive terminals.

## Principle

Connection and integrity remain primary. Etherflow compresses the visible surface while staying wired to the real event objects produced by transmission and execution.
